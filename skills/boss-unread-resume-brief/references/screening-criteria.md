# Screening Criteria Template

Use this reference when the user gives rough job requirements and needs help turning them into A/B/C criteria.

## Clarify Before Reading

Ask or infer:

- target position name;
- must-have experience;
- strong-match keywords;
- adjacent acceptable backgrounds;
- deal breakers;
- seniority threshold;
- language or location requirements;
- whether education is hard, soft, or only a risk flag.

## A/B/C Structure

### A

Candidate should be prioritized when the online resume shows:

- clear evidence for most must-have requirements;
- relevant project or production context;
- seniority at or above the target level;
- impact beyond individual execution, such as roadmap, team leadership, cross-team delivery, or adoption;
- no major unresolved deal breaker.

### B

Candidate is worth a phone screen when:

- one dimension is strong but another is missing;
- background is adjacent but plausibly transferable;
- seniority is close but not proven;
- evidence is promising but needs validation.

### C

Candidate is low priority when:

- must-have evidence is absent;
- background is too far from the target job;
- seniority is clearly below threshold;
- resume is mostly business, sales, operations, QA, or pure project coordination when the role requires technical/R&D judgment;
- online resume could not be read and chat summary is insufficient.

## Report Language

For every candidate, write:

- classification: A/B/C;
- evidence: exact resume-derived signals, paraphrased;
- risk: missing or weak requirement;
- next step: priority phone screen, optional phone screen, hold, or manual review.

Never imply the candidate was rejected on BOSS unless the user explicitly confirmed and the action was performed.
